# 🤖 Agentic AI Sends Compute Demand "Parabolic"

## Cause
- Nvidia's data center revenue **nearly doubled** year-over-year to $75.2 billion, with CEO Jensen Huang declaring demand has gone **"parabolic"** thanks to agentic AI workloads.
- SpaceX disclosed that **most of its Q1 capex went to AI**, signaling that even non-tech industrials are now massive compute buyers.
- Jeff Bezos publicly defended the AI investment cycle, arguing that **even a bubble would leave healthy infrastructure** behind — reinforcing the "spend now" mindset across hyperscalers.
- An $80 billion Nvidia buyback plus a dividend hike shows the company is **flush with cash** and confident the spending wave continues.

## Positive Impact
- **Nvidia (NVDA)** ➡️ Remains the dominant **picks-and-shovels** play; agentic AI multiplies inference cycles per query, expanding the addressable market.
- **Hyperscaler suppliers (Broadcom, Arista, Vertiv, Eaton)** ➡️ Benefit from **networking, power, and cooling** buildouts required to support exploding GPU clusters.
- **TSMC and SK Hynix** ➡️ Capture upside as **advanced packaging and HBM memory** remain the binding constraints on AI chip supply.
- **Power utilities (Constellation, Vistra, NextEra)** ➡️ Data centers are turning into **utility-scale electricity customers**, structurally lifting demand.

## Negative Impact
- **Legacy enterprise software (Intuit-style incumbents)** ➡️ Intuit's **17% workforce cut** signals that agentic AI is already cannibalizing seat-based SaaS revenue.
- **IT services firms (Cognizant, Infosys, Accenture)** ➡️ Code-writing agents threaten the **billable-hour outsourcing model**.
- **Cash-burning AI startups without moats** ➡️ Compute costs are **rising faster than monetization**, squeezing margins as Nvidia keeps pricing power.

# 🚀 SpaceX IPO Opens the Floodgates for a Public Space Economy

## Cause
- SpaceX filed its prospectus to list on the Nasdaq under ticker **SPCX**, in what's expected to be a **record-setting IPO**.
- The company cited a **$28.5 trillion total addressable market**, most of it adjacent to its current launch business (think Starlink, defense, lunar logistics).
- Musk retains **85% voting control**, signaling a founder-led growth narrative similar to Meta or Alphabet.
- **OpenAI may file confidentially as soon as tomorrow**, suggesting a re-opening of the mega-cap tech IPO window.

## Positive Impact
- **Aerospace suppliers (Rocket Lab, Redwire, AST SpaceMobile)** ➡️ A successful SpaceX listing **re-rates valuations** across the entire space sector.
- **Investment banks (Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan)** ➡️ Tier-one underwriters will pocket **massive fees** from SpaceX and OpenAI listings.
- **Nasdaq (NDAQ)** ➡️ Wins **listing fees and trading volume** from what could be the largest tech IPO ever.
- **Defense primes (Lockheed Martin, L3Harris)** ➡️ Stand to benefit as a public SpaceX **legitimizes commercial-military space partnerships**.

## Negative Impact
- **Traditional launch providers (Boeing/ULA, Arianespace)** ➡️ A capital-flush public SpaceX will **widen its cost advantage** and pressure legacy contracts.
- **Terrestrial telecoms (AT&T, Verizon)** ➡️ Starlink's accelerated rollout threatens **rural broadband and direct-to-cell** revenue streams.
- **Other late-stage unicorns** ➡️ Risk being **starved of capital** as institutional money concentrates in marquee IPO names.

# 💸 Stagflation-Lite: The Consumer Cracks While the Fed Stays Hawkish

## Cause
- Walmart issued a **weaker-than-expected outlook**, with the CFO warning that **fading tax refunds and high gas prices** are squeezing shoppers.
- E.l.f. Beauty is **rolling back tariff-related price hikes** — a sign that pricing power is breaking at the low end.
- Fed minutes showed most officials think **higher rates may be needed** if the Iran war keeps inflation elevated.
- The April Fed meeting saw the **highest dissent since 1992**, signaling deep uncertainty on the policy path.

## Positive Impact
- **Discount and dollar retailers (Dollar General, TJX, Aldi)** ➡️ Capture **trade-down traffic** as middle-income consumers tighten spending.
- **Energy majors (Exxon, Chevron, ConocoPhillips)** ➡️ Persistent geopolitical risk premium keeps **oil and refining margins elevated**.
- **Money-market funds and short-duration Treasuries** ➡️ A **higher-for-longer rate path** keeps cash yields attractive versus risk assets.
- **Defense contractors (Raytheon/RTX, Northrop Grumman)** ➡️ Ongoing Iran tensions **support order books and budget tailwinds**.

## Negative Impact
- **Big-box and mass retailers (Walmart, Target, Kohl's)** ➡️ Caught between **margin compression and consumer pullback**.
- **Consumer discretionary (autos, RV makers, casual dining)** ➡️ High rates plus stretched households **kill big-ticket demand**.
- **Regional banks** ➡️ A prolonged inverted curve squeezes **net interest margins** and pressures commercial real estate exposure.
- **Long-duration growth equities** ➡️ A 30-year yield near a **19-year high** raises the discount rate on far-future cash flows.

# 🇨🇳 The U.S.–China Tech Decoupling Hits an Inflection Point

## Cause
- Huang admitted Nvidia has **"largely conceded"** China's AI chip market to **Huawei** under U.S. export restrictions.
- This is a structural shift: the world's #2 economy is being **forced to build a parallel AI stack**.
- Tariff turbulence — visible in E.l.f.'s pricing commentary — is **fragmenting global supply chains** further.
- China's domestic semiconductor and AI ecosystem now has **billions in guaranteed demand** with no Western competition.

## Positive Impact
- **Huawei suppliers and SMIC** ➡️ Become the **default beneficiaries** of Chinese AI capex, even with process-node limitations.
- **U.S. semiconductor equipment makers serving non-China customers (Applied Materials, Lam Research, KLA ex-China)** ➡️ Benefit from **reshored fab capacity** in the U.S., Japan, and Europe.
- **Indian and Southeast Asian manufacturers** ➡️ Capture **supply-chain diversification** flows out of China.
- **Cybersecurity firms (Palo Alto, CrowdStrike)** ➡️ A bifurcated tech world **multiplies attack surfaces** and compliance spend.

## Negative Impact
- **Nvidia's China revenue line** ➡️ A **permanent structural haircut** to what was once a top growth market.
- **Apple and Tesla** ➡️ Heavy China exposure makes them **vulnerable to retaliatory measures** and consumer nationalism.
- **Global chip-equipment firms with China concentration (ASML, Tokyo Electron)** ➡️ Face **export-license overhangs** on a large chunk of their backlog.
- **Multinational consumer brands** ➡️ Tariff whiplash, like E.l.f.'s pricing reversal, **compresses margins** and complicates planning.
