# 🛢️ Iran Deal Reopens Strait of Hormuz, Triggering Energy Repricing

## Cause
- President Trump announced an **interim deal with Iran** to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, with a formal signing expected Friday and **toll-free passage** for roughly 60 days.
- **Qatar Energies** plans to ramp LNG output to **50% capacity in month one** and **80% by month two**, signaling supplier confidence in restored shipping lanes.
- **Goldman Sachs** projects Persian Gulf exports will hit **pre-war levels by end of July**, and both Goldman and Morgan Stanley have **cut price outlooks** for upcoming quarters.
- Brent crude is sliding to **$81.39** and WTI to **$78.87**, marking oil's **longest losing streak of the year** (four straight sessions).

## Positive Impact
- ⛴️ **Global shipping & tanker operators** (Frontline, Euronav, Maersk) — Reopened lanes mean **higher transit volumes** and a normalization of insurance premiums that had spiked during the conflict.
- 🏭 **LNG buyers and Asian utilities** (JERA, Korea Gas, Tokyo Gas) — Restored **Qatari LNG flows** translate to cheaper feedstock and relief for energy-import-dependent economies like Japan, which sources **90%+ of oil** from the Middle East.
- ✈️ **Airlines and logistics** (Delta, United, FedEx, UPS) — Falling **jet fuel costs** directly expand margins after months of war-driven premiums.
- 🛒 **Consumer discretionary and retail** (Walmart, Target, Amazon) — Lower energy costs ease **inflation pressure on households**, freeing up discretionary spending.

## Negative Impact
- 🛢️ **US shale producers** (Pioneer, Diamondback, Occidental) — A return to **pre-war price levels** squeezes breakeven economics for higher-cost domestic drillers.
- ⚡ **Integrated oil majors** (ExxonMobil, Chevron, Shell, BP) — Compressed crude prices weigh on **upstream earnings** and could delay capex programs.
- 🔧 **Oilfield services** (Halliburton, Schlumberger, Baker Hughes) — Lower prices typically trigger **reduced drilling activity** and pricing pressure on service contracts.
- 🌞 **Alternative energy & EV names** (First Solar, Enphase, Rivian) — Cheaper fossil fuels **erode the relative economics** of clean energy adoption at the margin.

# 💰 The "Magnificent Seven" Becomes Eight: SpaceX Joins the Mega-Cap Club

## Cause
- **SpaceX** is up **10.9% in pre-market** trading, extending a **~40% gain** across its first two public sessions.
- The Elon Musk–led company now carries a **$2.5 trillion+ market cap**, ranking among the **six biggest companies in the world**.
- JPMorgan's ECM co-head David Bower called SpaceX **"generationally transformational,"** signaling strong institutional appetite even at elevated levels.
- Musk's personal wealth jumped past **$1.27 trillion** (first trillionaire), and the **500 richest people added $336 billion** in a single day — the **largest one-day gain ever** on the Bloomberg Billionaires Index.

## Positive Impact
- 🚀 **Space-economy suppliers** (Rocket Lab, AST SpaceMobile, Iridium) — A blockbuster SpaceX listing **re-rates valuations** across the entire space sector and unlocks capital for peers.
- 🛰️ **Satellite communications & defense primes** (Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, L3Harris) — Increased **Starlink/Starshield demand** and government space budgets create durable contract pipelines.
- 🏦 **Investment banks** (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley) — A hot IPO market generates **underwriting and advisory fees** and re-opens the window for delayed listings.
- 📈 **Index funds and passive ETFs** (Vanguard, BlackRock) — Inclusion of a **$2.5T new name** drives forced buying and **AUM growth** in mega-cap-weighted products.

## Negative Impact
- 📡 **Legacy satellite operators** (Viasat, EchoStar, SES) — Starlink's expanding **broadband dominance** continues to erode their addressable market.
- 🛬 **Traditional launch competitors** (Boeing, Arianespace, ULA) — SpaceX's **cost advantage and reusability** keep pressuring legacy launch pricing and market share.
- 🌐 **Terrestrial telecom** (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) — **Direct-to-cell satellite service** threatens rural and roaming revenue streams long term.
- 💼 **Other 2026 IPO hopefuls** — A blowout SpaceX deal **absorbs investor attention and capital**, raising the bar for less differentiated listings.

# 🏦 Global Monetary Divergence: BOJ Hikes While Fed Eyes Cuts

## Cause
- The **Bank of Japan** raised rates **25 bps to 1%**, a **31-year high** not seen since 1995.
- The BOJ signaled it will **keep raising** depending on Middle East impact and inflation, with oil being especially consequential given Japan's **90%+ Middle East import reliance**.
- **45% of 74 central banks** surveyed plan to **buy more gold** in the coming year, signaling diversification away from dollar reserves.
- US **Strategic Petroleum Reserve** has fallen to its **lowest level since 1983**, with **172 million barrels** being released — limiting future US monetary/energy flexibility.

## Positive Impact
- 🏯 **Japanese banks** (Mitsubishi UFJ, Sumitomo Mitsui, Mizuho) — A steeper yield curve and higher rates **expand net interest margins** after decades of zero-rate compression.
- 💴 **Japanese yen and JGB short positioning** — Rate hikes **support the yen** and could reverse years of carry-trade outflows.
- 🥇 **Gold miners and bullion ETFs** (Newmont, Barrick, GLD) — Central bank buying provides a **structural demand floor**, with gold already at **$4,367/oz**.
- 🏛️ **Custody and reserve management firms** (State Street, BNY Mellon) — Rising **gold reserve allocations** boost vaulting and custody fee streams.

## Negative Impact
- 🏠 **Japanese real estate and REITs** — Higher borrowing costs **compress cap rates** and challenge a sector built on ultra-cheap financing.
- 💹 **Global carry-trade beneficiaries** (EM high-yielders, risk assets) — A **stronger yen** could unwind trillions in yen-funded leveraged positions, sparking volatility.
- 🚗 **Japanese exporters** (Toyota, Sony, Honda) — A **firmer yen** erodes translated overseas earnings and competitive pricing power.
- 🇺🇸 **US Treasury market** — Reduced **Japanese demand** for US bonds as JGB yields rise could pressure long-end Treasury pricing.
