# ⚖️ Trump 2.0 Tariff Wall Rebuilt After Supreme Court Setback

## Cause
- The **US Supreme Court ruled** much of President Trump's original global tariff regime **unconstitutional**, forcing the administration to find new legal pathways.
- The White House is now proposing a new tariff of **at least 10%** on imports from **60 trading partners**, framed around an investigation into **forced labor** in supply chains.
- Bloomberg reports the administration "signaled very clearly" it would **rebuild the tariff wall** as a centerpiece of foreign economic policy.
- New tariffs are subject to a **public comment and review period**, meaning implementation is delayed but the direction is set.
- This signals a **structural, not cyclical** shift toward US protectionism extending into 2026 and beyond.

## Positive Impact
- **US domestic manufacturers (Nucor, Steel Dynamics, Cleveland-Cliffs)** ➡️ Higher import costs **shield domestic producers** and support pricing power.
- **Onshoring beneficiaries (Caterpillar, Rockwell Automation, Emerson)** ➡️ Companies enabling **US reshoring** of factories see stronger capex pipelines.
- **Trade compliance & supply chain software (SAP, Descartes Systems)** ➡️ Importers will need **new systems** to audit forced-labor exposure across 60 countries.
- **Mexican near-shoring plays** ➡️ As Asian exporters face tariffs, **Mexico-based assembly** may capture redirected production flows.

## Negative Impact
- **Big-box retailers (Walmart, Target, Dollar Tree)** ➡️ Heavy reliance on **imported low-cost goods** means **margin compression** or price hikes that hurt volumes.
- **Apparel and footwear (Nike, Lululemon, Gap)** ➡️ Sourcing concentrated in **tariffed Asian countries** drives up COGS.
- **Auto OEMs with global supply chains (Ford, GM, Stellantis)** ➡️ A **10% blanket tariff** raises input costs on parts that cross borders multiple times.
- **Consumer electronics (Apple, Best Buy)** ➡️ Hardware **margin pressure** as device components face new duties.
- **Container shipping & global logistics (Maersk, FedEx)** ➡️ Reduced **trade volumes** historically follow broad tariff escalations.

## Cause
- The **US and Iran are exchanging strikes** even as both sides publicly insist the ceasefire holds — the US hit Iranian **radar and command sites**, while Iran targeted a US airbase.
- Iranian missiles and drones reportedly **struck Bahrain and Kuwait**, with at least **one killed** at Kuwait's international airport.
- **Bahrain** hosts the **US Fifth Fleet**, raising the stakes of any escalation.
- Negotiations are stuck on **freedom of navigation through the Strait of Hormuz** — a chokepoint for roughly **20% of global oil**.
- Former Secretary of State **Mike Pompeo** says it's "very likely" the US will have to **re-arm Israel** and resume kinetic strikes.

## Positive Impact
- **Defense primes (Lockheed Martin, RTX, Northrop Grumman, General Dynamics)** ➡️ Pompeo's call for **more weaponry to Israel** points to sustained **munitions reorders**.
- **Oil majors (ExxonMobil, Chevron, ConocoPhillips)** ➡️ Hormuz risk supports a **geopolitical premium** in crude prices.
- **US LNG exporters (Cheniere, Venture Global)** ➡️ Middle East instability boosts **European and Asian demand** for non-Gulf gas.
- **Cybersecurity firms (Palo Alto Networks, CrowdStrike, Fortinet)** ➡️ Heightened risk of **Iranian state-sponsored cyber retaliation** against US critical infrastructure.
- **Gold and safe-haven assets** ➡️ Ongoing flare-ups support **gold miners (Newmont, Barrick)** and Treasury demand.

## Negative Impact
- **Global airlines (Emirates, Qatar Airways, Delta, United)** ➡️ Airport strikes and **Gulf airspace closures** disrupt long-haul routes.
- **Container shipping (Maersk, Hapag-Lloyd)** ➡️ Hormuz and Red Sea risks **lengthen routes** and inflate insurance costs.
- **Asian oil importers (India, Japan, South Korea refiners)** ➡️ Dependence on **Gulf crude** means margin squeeze if prices spike.
- **Tourism and hospitality in the Gulf** ➡️ **Bahrain, Kuwait, UAE** see weaker visitor flows amid security concerns.

- The Trump administration's plan to send exposed Americans to a **Kenyan facility** is blocked by a **Kenyan court**.
- Dr. **Mehmet Oz** says transporting patients back to the US is "**not the wisest move**," citing UK and German alternatives.
- Lack of pre-positioned **biocontainment capacity** is exposing gaps in **pandemic response infrastructure**.
- The outbreak revives investor focus on **vaccines, diagnostics, and therapeutics** for hemorrhagic fevers.

## Positive Impact
- **Vaccine makers (Merck, Johnson & Johnson)** ➡️ Merck's **Ervebo Ebola vaccine** sees **resurgent procurement demand** from governments and WHO.
