# 🤖 AI Infrastructure Boom Enters the Inference Era

## Cause
- **Nvidia** continues to post **near-80% revenue growth** with **75% margins**, with hyperscaler cloud customers accounting for **more than 50%** of revenue.
- The AI compute cycle is shifting from **model training** to **inference**, where real-world applications generate sustained, recurring chip demand.
- **Alibaba** guided toward **$100 billion in cloud revenue at 20% margins** within five years, with AI revenue projected to grow at **triple-digit rates** and exceed **50% of cloud revenue** within a year.
- China's **token demand is set to quadruple** in 2026, with over **70% tied to inference**, accelerating the domestic AI application layer (coding, agentic tools, generative video).

## Positive Impact
- **Nvidia (NVDA)** 💎 — Still dominant in training and well-positioned in inference, with all major hyperscaler customers already confirming **elevated capex** for chips this year.
- **Alibaba (BABA)** ☁️ — Cloud growth **accelerated to 40% year-on-year** and the company is the clearest Chinese beneficiary of the **Model-as-a-Service (MaaS)** monetization wave.
- **Data center construction & power suppliers** ⚡ — Industrial firms supplying **transformers, cooling, and heavy equipment** (including **John Deere**'s construction segment) benefit from the build-out wave.
- **Chinese domestic chipmakers** 🇨🇳 — With China pursuing **100% self-sufficiency in inference chips**, local foundries and designers gain a **structurally captive market**.
- **Asian tech exporters** 🌏 — Japan's exports rose nearly **12% in March**, fueled by a **30% surge in chip exports**, lifting equipment makers and semiconductor suppliers across Japan and South Korea.

## Negative Impact
- **Custom silicon challengers** — Hyperscaler in-house chips and AI startups still show **no evidence of denting Nvidia's market share**, making it hard to justify their R&D spend.
- **Legacy SaaS vendors in China** — Alibaba notes China **lacked a meaningful SaaS industry**, so enterprises are leapfrogging directly to **agentic AI**, bypassing traditional software incumbents.
- **Pure model-training plays** — Training is becoming a **commoditized service** with limited pricing power, pressuring firms that haven't pivoted to differentiated inference capabilities.

# ⛽ Middle East Conflict Reshapes Global Energy & Travel Costs

## Cause
- The **Iran war**, which began in February, has caused jet fuel costs to **more than double**, with the **Strait of Hormuz** still largely blocked.
- Bloomberg Intelligence forecasts **2026 fuel cost increases of 27% for IAG and Air France-KLM**, and **22% for Lufthansa**.
- **Airfares are running ~50% higher year-on-year** heading into peak summer season, with some routes seeing a **doubling of ticket prices**.
- **EasyJet** has guided to a **first-half loss of £540–560 million**, citing a **£25 million** fuel bill hit so far — with more pain expected in later quarters.
- **Japan's producer prices rose 4.9%**, a three-to-four-year high, partly driven by the new oil shock.

## Positive Impact
- **Ryanair (RYAAY)** ✈️ — Strong **fuel hedging** insulates near-term earnings, and it may capture passengers **trading down from long-haul to intra-European trips**.
- **US energy exporters** 🛢️ — With Europe and Asia scrambling for **alternative jet fuel supplies**, American refiners and LNG/oil exporters gain pricing leverage.
- **Well-capitalized airlines globally** — Stronger balance sheets enable **acquisitions of weaker carriers**, echoing the **Spirit Airlines** collapse and accelerating industry consolidation.
- **Bank of Japan rate hike beneficiaries** 🏦 — Imported energy inflation strengthens the case for a **June rate hike** (market pricing in **>75% odds**), supporting Japanese banks and insurers.

## Negative Impact
- **EasyJet (EZJ)** — Already warned of widening losses, and as a price-sensitive low-cost carrier, it **cannot fully pass higher fuel costs onto customers**.
- **IAG, Air France-KLM, Lufthansa** — Facing the **steepest cost hikes**, with full-year guidance cuts increasingly likely.
- **London Heathrow & European hubs** — April passenger volumes fell **5.3% to 6.7 million**, signaling weakening transit traffic through major airports.
- **Japanese household consumption** 🇯🇵 — Q1 consumption is tracking near its **weakest reading in recent quarters**, as inflation-fatigued consumers pull back.

# 🛒 The K-Shaped Consumer Splits Retail Winners and Losers

## Cause
- The **K-shaped economy** narrative is intensifying, with lower-income households squeezed while upper-income spending holds firm.
- **Walmart** is expected to post **$748 billion in revenue** with **same-store sales gains of 4–5%**, reflecting **trade-down behavior** toward value retailers.
- **Home Depot's** $178 billion revenue forecast is being pressured by **30-year mortgage rates near 5%**, deterring large home renovation projects.
- **Tariffs remain alive and well** — retailers including Home Depot are flagging tariff management as a key operational priority, even as headlines have shifted to other stories.
- **Job security concerns and inflation uncertainty** continue to weigh on discretionary spending categories.

## Positive Impact
- **Walmart (WMT)** 🛍️ — Best positioned in **value and convenience**, gaining **market share** and showing sustained e-commerce momentum and improving online margins.
- **Discount grocers & dollar stores** — Beneficiaries as middle-income shoppers **trade down** for everyday essentials.
- **Premium travel and luxury** ✈️ — The same K-shaped split lifts **private aviation, business class, and high-end leisure** as wealthier consumers keep spending.

## Negative Impact
- **Home Depot (HD)** 🔨 — **Big-ticket remodels are being deferred** as high mortgage rates and economic uncertainty crimp homeowner budgets.
- **John Deere (DE)** 🚜 — Facing **"farmageddon"** — tariffs on agricultural exports, a tough planting season, and **elevated used tractor inventories** that weigh on new equipment demand and pricing.
- **Mid-tier discretionary retailers** — Squeezed from above (premium brands holding firm) and below (Walmart/dollar stores winning value shoppers), with little room to maneuver.
- **Mortgage-sensitive home goods suppliers** — Furniture, appliances, and renovation-linked vendors face prolonged demand weakness while the **10-year Treasury yield hovers near 5%**.
