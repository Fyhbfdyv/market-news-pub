# SpaceX’s First Earnings Report Will Test Investor Appetite for Commercial Space 🚀

## Cause

- SpaceX will publish its **first earnings report** on Aug. 4, giving investors their first detailed look at growth, margins, and cash flow.
- At least **20% of locked-up shares** may become tradable on Aug. 6, potentially increasing selling pressure.
- Short positions represent nearly **one-third of publicly tradable shares**, creating the potential for sharp price swings.
- Goldman Sachs is expanding access to **pre-IPO companies**, showing strong demand for private-market exposure.

## Positive Impact

- **Goldman Sachs and alternative asset managers** could benefit from fees earned by connecting wealthy investors with private growth companies.
- **Satellite and aerospace suppliers** may gain if strong SpaceX results confirm continued spending on rockets, satellites, and ground infrastructure.
- **SpaceX shareholders** could benefit from a short squeeze if earnings exceed expectations and bearish investors rush to close positions.
- **Commercial space competitors** such as Rocket Lab could receive higher valuations if SpaceX demonstrates attractive industry economics.

## Negative Impact

- **SpaceX shares** could face heavy selling when the lock-up expires, especially after their weak performance since the market debut.
- **Short sellers** may suffer large losses if positive earnings trigger a rapid rebound in the limited share float.
- **Traditional satellite operators** could face greater pricing pressure if Starlink continues expanding its broadband network.
- **Government-focused aerospace contractors** may lose selected launch and satellite contracts as SpaceX strengthens its cost advantage.

# The Iran Conflict Is Creating a New Oil-Price Shock 🛢️

## Cause

- Brent crude has risen above **$95 per barrel** following 11 consecutive nights of U.S. strikes on Iran.
- The conflict raises fears of supply disruptions around the **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical route for global energy shipments.
- Diplomatic progress appears limited, keeping a larger **geopolitical risk premium** in oil prices.
- Higher energy costs are weighing on stocks because they can increase inflation and delay interest-rate cuts.

## Positive Impact

- **Exxon Mobil, Chevron, Shell, and BP** may earn stronger upstream profits because their oil production becomes more valuable at higher prices.
- **ConocoPhillips and other exploration companies** could generate more free cash flow due to their direct exposure to crude prices.
- **Oilfield-service companies** such as SLB and Halliburton may benefit if producers increase drilling and development budgets.
- **Tanker operators** could receive higher shipping rates if vessels must take longer or safer routes around disrupted areas.
- **Defense contractors** such as RTX and Lockheed Martin may see stronger demand for air defense, missiles, and surveillance systems.

## Negative Impact

- **Airlines** such as Delta, United, and American could face higher jet-fuel expenses that are difficult to pass fully to passengers.
- **FedEx, UPS, and trucking companies** may experience margin pressure as transportation fuel costs rise.
- **Chemical and packaging producers** could pay more for petroleum-based raw materials and energy-intensive manufacturing.
- **Consumer-discretionary companies** may see weaker demand as households spend more on gasoline and utilities.
- **Interest-rate-sensitive sectors** such as homebuilders and real estate could suffer if the oil shock keeps inflation elevated.

# A New U.S. Tariff Wave Could Reshape Global Supply Chains 🌎

## Cause

- The White House may announce another round of **import duties** after earlier tariffs were struck down by the Supreme Court.
- Temporary replacement tariffs are approaching expiration, increasing pressure on the administration to find another legal route.
- Potential action against Canada and other trading partners creates uncertainty for companies with **cross-border supply chains**.
- Businesses may accelerate efforts to move production closer to U.S. customers or into countries with lower tariff exposure.

## Positive Impact

- **U.S. steel and aluminum producers** such as Nucor and Steel Dynamics could gain pricing power if tariffs make imported metals more expensive.
- **Domestic manufacturers** may become more competitive when foreign products face higher border costs.
- **Industrial automation companies** such as Rockwell Automation could benefit as businesses build or modernize U.S. factories.
- **Railroads and warehouse operators** may gain from increased North American manufacturing and regional supply-chain activity.
- **Supply-chain software providers** could see stronger demand as companies redesign sourcing and track tariff exposure.

## Negative Impact

- **Automakers** such as Ford, General Motors, and Stellantis may face higher costs because vehicles and parts regularly cross North American borders.
- **Retailers** such as Walmart and Target could experience margin pressure from higher prices on imported consumer goods.
- **Construction companies and homebuilders** may pay more for lumber, metals, appliances, and other building materials.
- **Canadian exporters** could lose U.S. market share if new duties make their products less competitive.
- **Small businesses** may be especially vulnerable because they have less bargaining power and fewer options for changing suppliers.

# Advanced AI Is Becoming Both a Cybersecurity Tool and a Systemic Risk 🤖🔐

## Cause

- The Federal Reserve warned banks that advanced AI models can identify **software weaknesses and security vulnerabilities**.
- The Fed’s delayed access to Anthropic’s model highlights a growing capability gap between regulators and private AI companies.
- OpenAI and Anthropic are increasing **federal lobbying spending** as policymakers consider AI security and oversight rules.
- Financial institutions must balance the productivity benefits of AI against risks involving data leakage, fraud, and automated cyberattacks.

## Positive Impact

- **Cybersecurity companies** such as Palo Alto Networks, CrowdStrike, and Zscaler could see greater demand for AI-based threat detection and protection.
- **Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, OpenAI, and Anthropic** may benefit as regulated industries seek secure enterprise AI services.
- **Accenture and other technology consultants** could win projects helping banks adopt AI under stronger governance controls.
- **Cloud infrastructure and data-center providers** may gain from the computing requirements of advanced security models.
- **Model-testing and compliance businesses** could develop a growing market for independent AI audits and risk monitoring.

## Negative Impact

- **Banks and payment companies** may face higher security, compliance, and insurance costs as AI-enabled attacks become more sophisticated.
- **Smaller financial institutions** could struggle because they lack the budgets and technical talent needed to evaluate advanced models.
- **AI developers** may encounter slower product launches or higher operating costs if regulators impose strict testing and access requirements.
- **Legacy cybersecurity vendors** could lose relevance if their products cannot detect rapidly changing AI-generated threats.
- **Companies with weak data controls** face greater legal and reputational risk from confidential information entering external AI systems.

# America’s Skilled-Trades Shortage Is Driving Investment in Vocational Talent 🛠️

## Cause

- Google, BlackRock, Carhartt, and Ford have joined an initiative focused on the shortage of **technicians, builders, and other tradespeople**.
- The alliance plans to work with unions and trade groups while investing in **apprenticeship programs**.
- Retirements and limited vocational training have reduced the supply of workers needed for construction, manufacturing, and infrastructure.
- Factory expansion, data-center construction, and energy projects are increasing demand for electricians, mechanics, and technicians.

## Positive Impact

- **Vocational schools and workforce-training providers** could receive more enrollment, corporate partnerships, and program funding.
- **Ford and other manufacturers** may reduce production bottlenecks by developing a larger pipeline of qualified technicians.
- **Construction and engineering firms** could complete more projects if apprenticeship programs expand the available labor pool.
- **Tool and workwear companies** such as Stanley Black & Decker and Carhartt may benefit from growth in the trades workforce.
- **Infrastructure and data-center developers** could gain from improved access to electricians, welders, and equipment specialists.

## Negative Impact

- **Employers that do not invest in training** may face continuing wage inflation, vacant positions, and project delays.
- **Labor-intensive construction companies** could experience weaker margins until the worker shortage meaningfully improves.
- **Traditional four-year colleges** may face enrollment pressure if more students choose lower-cost apprenticeships with direct career paths.
- **Manufacturers in high-cost regions** could struggle to expand if skilled labor remains scarce despite national training programs.
- **Automation-resistant service businesses** may continue facing higher labor costs because many skilled physical tasks cannot easily be replaced by AI.
